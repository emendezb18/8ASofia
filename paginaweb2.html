Laos


History and Language

Laos has different groups of people who speak different languages. The main language is Lao, but in the past, they also used languages like French and English. After wars, the government grouped people based on language and where they live. They have three main groups: Lao Loum (Lowland Lao), Lao Theung (Lao of the Mountain Slopes), and Lao Soung (Lao of the Mountain Tops). Each group has its own language and way of life.

The largest group, Lao Loum, lives by the Mekong River and speaks Tai languages. Lao Theung, who make up about one-fourth of the population, speak Austroasiatic languages. Lao Soung, about one-tenth, speaks Hmong-Mien or Tibeto-Burman languages. Each group has its own traditions and social structure.


Religion

The main religion is Theravada Buddhism, approximately more than half of the country practices this religion, the other groups are Lao Theung and Lao Soung that are not Buddhist religions, and approximately 2/5 of the country practice this religion.

Besides these religions, Laos also has religion like Christians, Muslims and Baha, but they are not as practiced as Theravada Buddhism, and some people that immigrated from Vietnam to Laos brought another religion called Vietnamese which is a practice combined with Buddhism and Confucianism religion.
